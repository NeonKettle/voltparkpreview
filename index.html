FAQ

Common questions.

You probably have a few.

What if we don't get enough EV drivers?
At just 3 sessions per day — half our conservative estimate — the charger still covers its own repayment and generates positive cash flow. Installing a charger puts your venue on every EV mapping app in the country, attracting drivers who wouldn't have otherwise visited.
Who handles maintenance and breakdowns?
Voltpark does. The charger is remotely monitored 24/7 through our OCPP management platform. The Thunder 120C supports firmware-over-the-air (FOTA) updates and remote fault diagnosis; most issues are resolved without a site visit. If a hardware issue requires a physical response, we send a certified technician.
What does the finance look like and who's responsible for it?
The finance is a commercial chattel mortgage arranged through our broker network. The venue owns the asset from day one. Standard commercial credit checks apply. Approval typically takes 3 to 5 business days.
Is there a revenue share or ongoing commission to Voltpark?
No. Voltpark earns its margin on the hardware supply and installation. Once the charger is commissioned, 100% of the charging revenue belongs to the venue. No ongoing commissions, no revenue split, no hidden fees beyond the $100/month OCPP platform management fee.
How long does installation take?
From signed agreement to live charger, typically 4 to 8 weeks depending on site complexity and council requirements. Voltpark manages the full process through our certified electrician network.
What happens at the end of the 48 months?
You own the charger outright. At that point the repayment obligation ends and you keep 100% of revenue with no ongoing fixed cost beyond the platform fee. At 6 sessions per day that's approximately $1,565/month gross profit.
Which EVs does it work with?
Every EV currently sold in Australia. The Thunder 120C supports CCS2, CHAdeMO, GB/T, NACS, Type 1 AC, and Type 2 AC, covering BYD, Tesla (with adaptor), Kia, Hyundai, MG, Volvo, Polestar, Mercedes, BMW, and others.

The Charger

Grasen Thunder 120C.

AC/DC 3-in-1 commercial EV charger.

The Thunder 120C is a commercial-grade AC/DC fast charger designed for destination venues. It handles up to three vehicles simultaneously — two DC fast charge ports and one AC port — in a single compact footprint. One unit. Three revenue streams. One set of installation costs.

Up to 120kW DC output across two ports. Charges a typical EV from 20% to 80% in 30–40 minutes — fast enough to drive a stop, slow enough to drive a spend.
